# Facebook Style Guide

Updated: 2026-07-10
Source style adapted from: `mayaobongro.vn/facebook/STYLE-GUIDE.md`
Target brand: `mayaopickleball.vn`

## Purpose

This guide captures the posting style we want for `mayaopickleball.vn`, using
the Facebook sales rhythm from the source guide and adapting it fully for custom
pickleball apparel.

Do not copy another page's wording or design 1:1. Reuse the structure, rhythm,
visual hierarchy, and sales logic.

## Core Style

The page should sell with the image first, caption second.

Target pattern:
- The post image already contains the main sales message.
- The image often looks like a mini landing page or sales sheet.
- Benefits are shown visually in short blocks, not buried in long text.
- Contact details and trust signals are repeated on the image itself.
- Captions open with a clear angle, not a generic greeting.
- Captions are emotional-commercial, but still easy to skim.

For our page, each post should feel like:
- a premium pickleball apparel showcase;
- a short custom-uniform sales pitch;
- a proof-of-quality post for teams, clubs, academies, schools, companies, and
  court communities.

## Visual Direction

### 1. Preferred image types

Rotate between these 3 formats:

1. Sales-sheet image
   Use a collage with:
   - hero shirt / full set view;
   - 2-4 closeups;
   - short benefit labels;
   - hotline / website;
   - trust badges.

2. Model-wearing-apparel image set
   Use generated images with watermark when the pose, fit, sleeve/collar style,
   or team identity is the main selling point.

3. Mixed proof set
   Combine:
   - front/back apparel view;
   - fabric or print closeup;
   - pickleball court context;
   - a short headline inside the image.

### 2. What should appear on-image

Whenever possible, put these directly in the visual:
- team, club, court, or concept name;
- 3-5 product benefits;
- custom service promise;
- website or hotline;
- 1-3 trust markers.

Recommended benefit vocabulary:
- vai the thao thoang mat;
- in logo, ten, so sac net, ben mau;
- form gon khi van dong tren san;
- ao co co / co tron / khong tay;
- phoi nam nu dong bo;
- thiet ke theo yeu cau;
- du size cho team;
- duong may chac chan;
- bao hanh san pham.

### 3. Image tone

Aim for:
- bold contrast;
- premium court-sport feel;
- clear apparel focus;
- readable typography;
- organized composition;
- energetic but not chaotic color.

Avoid:
- plain raw image dumps with no selling layer;
- too many tiny text blocks;
- generic tennis/badminton visuals that make the sport unclear;
- running/race cues;
- soft pastel lifestyle look;
- messy mixed-color layouts with no focal point.

## Caption Style

### 1. Caption role

The caption is not the whole sale. Its job is to:
- create the angle;
- explain who the set is for;
- reinforce quality and customization;
- push contact action softly.

### 2. Caption structure

Use this order most of the time:

1. Hook
   One strong opening line.

2. Short context
   Say who this set suits or what makes it stand out.

3. Benefit block
   Usually 3 short bullets or 1 short compact paragraph.

4. Custom service line
   Mention logo, color, number/name, team concept, collar/sleeve style, or size
   range.

5. CTA
   Website + hotline/Zalo.

6. Hashtags
   Keep short and repeatable.

### 3. Tone

Target tone:
- direct;
- premium but not luxury-overdone;
- sales-aware;
- practical;
- confident;
- easy to skim on mobile.

Good traits to keep:
- opens with an idea, not filler;
- sounds like a brand that understands team apparel production;
- keeps momentum;
- sells confidence, identity, and quality.

Avoid:
- over-cute phrasing;
- chatty Facebook diary tone;
- long generic motivational copy;
- keyword stuffing;
- too many emojis;
- confusing pickleball with tennis, badminton, running, or gym wear.

### 4. Pronoun rule

Use `ben em` consistently in captions and sales lines.

Avoid mixing:
- `ben minh`
- `ben toi`
- `shop`

For this page, `ben em` should be the default voice in:
- custom service lines;
- CTA lines;
- reassurance or trust-building lines.

## Content Pillars For Mayaopickleball.vn

Use these pillars in rotation:

### Adult team / club posts

Angle ideas:
- form mac gon, len doi hinh dep khi ra san;
- phu hop CLB, nhom tap, giai phong trao, doi cong ty;
- phoi mau rieng cho team;
- in logo, ten, so theo yeu cau;
- ao co co hoac khong tay tuy style cua doi.

### Women / mixed team posts

Angle ideas:
- phoi nam nu dong bo nhung van dung form tung nguoi;
- mau sang, gon, de nhan dien tren san;
- ao nu co co / khong tay van lich su va nang dong;
- phu hop team ban be, CLB, nhom tap sau gio lam.

### Kids / student posts

Angle ideas:
- mau tuoi, de nhan dien;
- mac thoai mai khi van dong;
- phu hop truong hoc, lop nang khieu, doi thieu nhi;
- dong bo giua hoc sinh va giao vien / HLV.

### Court / academy posts

Angle ideas:
- cung concept cho hoc vien, HLV va nhom thi dau;
- dong bo nhan dien cho san pickleball hoac trung tam dao tao;
- nhieu size van giu cung chat va cung style;
- lam ao giai dau noi bo, su kien khai truong, mini league.

### Quality-detail posts

Angle ideas:
- chat vai;
- do thoang;
- duong may;
- in an;
- do ben khi choi ngoai troi / trong nha;
- cai nhin ky truoc khi xuat xuong.

### Team-story posts

Angle ideas:
- moi mau ao la mot ban sac rieng cua team;
- mot doi pickleball can bo do nhin ra chat rieng tren san;
- dong phuc khong chi de mac, ma de nhan dien va ket noi;
- ao dep giup team tu tin hon khi tap, giao huu, va thi dau.

## Recommended Post Mix

For a 10-day run, use a rhythm like:
- 3 adult team / club posts;
- 2 women or mixed-team posts;
- 2 kids / student posts;
- 2 court / academy posts;
- 1 detail / craftsmanship post.

Alternate between:
- collage sales sheet;
- model showcase;
- story-led proof post.
